Electronics & Communication Engineering

About The Department

The Department of Electronics and Communication engineering was started in the year 2007 with an intake of 60 students. The department has been recognized by AICTE New Delhi, affiliated to Visvesvaraya Technological University (VTU), Belgaum. The department consists of highly qualified and experienced faculty with good recognition in academia and industry. Department provides very good academic learning environment complemented by research work. The department offers B.E. degree with comprehensive curriculum on topics related to all aspects of electronics hardware and software.

Vision

“To promote knowledge acquisition and dissemination in the field of Electronics and Communication Engineering to impart quality education to students, empowering them to become successful Professionals”.

Mission

1. To provide sustainable and conducive environment for faculty and students in teaching learning process through interaction and cooperation.
2. To nurture budding engineers to be ethical, disciplined, employable Professionals.
3. To focus on Industry Institute Interaction to meet the requirements of the industry and develop the entrepreneurial skills.
4. To enable students to be competent, creative and confident to take up the profession and deliver with a social concern.

Infrastructure

The department has state of art infrastructure and good computing environment equipped and supported by high speed LAN. The Department is headed by Prof. Krishnananda L, Associate Professor in Electronics and Communication Engineering who has rich experience in teaching. The department consists of Four associate professors and Eight assistant professors. Some of the faculty members are working towards their Ph.D. The students are given good computing and internet facility and created good academic ambience.

Power Electronics Lab

This laboratory is to provide the students with a basic foundation for analysis, design, test, and control of power electronics converters by simulation and experimentation to acquire the knowledge of power semiconductor devices and their control. Most advanced kits and modules are sufficiently available to conduct the experiments on power devices. The laboratory also helps to understand the arrangement and topology of the circuits in which switch-mode devices are used. The Purposes of the laboratory experiments are to learn the proper use of instrumentation used as the various triggering circuits for different switching devices such as switching characteristics for SCR, TRIAC, IGBT, MOSFET, GTO, Thyristor, for choppers/Inverters and other commutating devices.
